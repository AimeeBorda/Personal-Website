About Me

I'm a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science and Statistics at Trinity College Dublin under the supervision of Prof. Vasileios Koutavas. In collaboration with Lero, my research focuses on the formal reasoning and representation of self-adaptive security systems. I am a member of the Foundations and Methods Group, which holds a weekly series of seminars on topics about formal methods and concurrency theory.

Research

Separation-Based Reasoning for Message Passing Programs

We study separation- based, local reasoning techniques fine-tuned to deterministic message-passing programs with channel reuse. We develop a Hoare-style proof system that allows us to prove the correctness of channel-based implementations for a class of Sorting Networks. Processes are separated according to the channel endpoints used, and this separation criteria is complicated by channel reuse patterns used. We also prove total correctness for our proof system.

Using a Generic Cloud for Heterogeneous Programming

We study numerous tech- niques for automating C programs parallelization and distribution. Through compiling tech- niques, we transform C programs to OpenCL programs and RMI. Thus utilizing all processing elements - CPU and GPU from available machines. We prove the correctness of our project through the implementation of a draft game where the next move is computed over different machines and processing elements.
